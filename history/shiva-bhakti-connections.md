## aShTavidha-bhakti
aShTavidha-bhakti is described in shiva-dharma, garuDa-purANa and the vaikhAnasa bhRgu saMhitA.

## Direction of borrowing
### S to V claim
Some claim that bhAgavata type bhakti was borrowed from shiva-dharma tradition. 

> If on one hand the extremely influential dharma literature of Shaiva-s shows a distinctive kind of devotion that is not found in Vaishnava dharma literature, the comparatively archaic Pancharatra literature, or texts of the older Vaishnava strata like the Narayaniya, it gives us evidence about where ideas are going. - Andra


### Text-compilation dating value 
These currently available compilations aren't evidence of priority for a given shloka. Even lore in bhAgavata was already present pre-ANDAL.



### garuDa purANa date
What's their argument for aShTavidha bhakti in shivadharma being older than in garuDa purANa? scholars date garuDa "core" to 4th to 11th CE. No valid reason to date it after shivadharma.

The reverse direction is easily attestable - tiruvempAvai being based on tiruppAvai https://youtu.be/fK1WXODxJP8 .

### shivadharma date
> Dating for the Shivadharma is discussed in Bisschop's Universal Shaivism and Mirning's Rudras on Earth. The influence of the Shivadharma and its uniqueness compared to the Vishnudharma is found in basically any major work of the Shivadharma project, but A Saiva Utopia is notable as well as the Sivadharmamrta. Comparisons with the Bhagavata have yet to be done systematically but are variously found in Schwartz's "Caught in the Net of Sastra" as well as his dissertation. - Andra

### ALvAr date
shivadharma is at best contemporaneous with the ALvAr, likely later. 


### Common ancestor claim
"S to V copy" theory sounds like "hinduism copied buddhism" redux (from the same people too). 

Now, given that the same idea occurs in the words of the ALvAr and in SD, a honest person cannot determine the direction of transmission, and will suppose a common ancestor. A biased person on the other hand ...

The shloka (and bhakti) were inherited by both shaiva-s and vaiShNava-s, independently of currently available compilations.
