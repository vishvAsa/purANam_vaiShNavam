It all starts with the invasion of Kanchipuram on October 11 , 1686. Fearing the invaders , the devotees and the then administrator family of the temple , Thathaachaarya took the utsavar idol to udaiyarpalayam forests.  Where the lord presided. They waited till Aurangazeb was dead on march 3 , 1707. Then the Thaathaacharya family approached the powerful general of the Hyderabad nizam to help get the utsavar idol back from udaiyarpalayam. The general name was Thodarmal , he was an ardent devotee of atthaan jeeyar. This jeeyar belonged to Thengalai sampradhaya while the thaathaacharyas were a distinct community adhering to the vadagalai sampradaya.  

The thengalai jeeyar took this opportunity to lay conditions that thengalai devotees must be given the right to chant prabhandhams inside the temple in return for helping get the utsavar murthi back to the temple.  The thaathaachaaryas agreed reluctantly but with a rider that their family will start and end the Prabhandham chanting. Here lies the hitch. The prabhandham paaraayanam  starts with verses praising the thengalai aacharya Sri manavaala maamaunigal , thaathaacharya was not comfortable doing this. So he gave that honor to his daughter’s son . It so happened this grandson had a family dispute and hence went to court and agreed to give his rights to thengalai devotees . 

Since then the cases have been going on in various courts and there have been many litigations.  

The present problem is mainly due to the govt wanting to enjoy plunder of the temple by provoking the problem between thengalai and vadakalai sects.
