**Chapter One**

1. The Lord, hailed by the world as **Ubaya Vibhūtinātha** (Lord of both worlds), has revealed His immense grace in this mundane realm, **Līlā Vibhūti**, more than in Śrī Vaikuṇṭham, the **Nitya Vibhūti**. Our Āḻvār [^3_1] has conveyed this truth through the pashuram "*Kaḍal ñālattu aḷi mikkan*" (Tiruvāymoḻi 4-8-5). The reason for the Lord's greater affection for this dark world, compared to **Tripād Vibhūti** where **Nitya Sūris** and **Muktas** constantly enjoy His full service, is primarily to alleviate the suffering of **Samsāris**. Also, the abundance of **Divya Desams** scattered across this earthly realm is a major factor. Among the hundreds of **Divya Desams** that flourish here, three are particularly revered as "**Kōyil**, **Tirumalai**, **Perumāḷ Kōyil**." These are traditionally known as **Śrīrangam**, **Tiruvēṅkaṭamalai**, and **Śrī Kāñcī**.

2. One might ask why these three **Divya Desams** hold special pre-eminence among all.

Eminent scholars state that it is because these three **Divya Desams** alone have been celebrated in the **Tiruviruttam**. It is noteworthy that **Śrīrangam** is glorified as "Porunīrttiruvarangā aruḷāy" (28), **Tiruvēṅkaṭamalai** as "Perumāṉmalaiyō Tiruvēṅkaṭam eṉṟu kaṟkiṉṟa vāsakamē" (60), and **Śrī Kāñcī** as "Viṇṇōr toḻuṅkaṇṇaṉ Veḵkāūtu" (25). Since other **Divya Desams** are not praised in the initial **Tiruviruttam**, Śrī Vaiṣṇava scholars uphold the notion that the Āḻvār's intention was to give primacy to these three **Divya Desams**.

3. Among the aforementioned three holy places, Kāñcīpuram enjoys paramount importance. The reason for this is the **Śrī Sūkti** of Āḻvār himself. Our Dēvapperumāḷ is glorified by Nammāḻvār in the very first pashuram of the initial **Tiruviruttam** and also in the first pashuram of the final **Tiruvāymoḻi**. In the first pashuram of **Tiruviruttam**, he said "Imayōr talaivā! Mey niṉṟu kēṭṭaruḷāy" (Oh, Lord of the celestials! Please listen with truth). The reference to "Imayōr talaivā!" directly refers to the Lord Dēvārāja. Similarly, in the first pashuram of **Tiruvāymoḻi**, "Amararkaḷ adhipati" also refers to Dēvapperumāḷ. His name "Praṇatārtiharana" (remover of suffering for those who surrender) is invoked when Nammāḻvār says "*Tūyaraṟu chuṭaraḍiyeṉṟu maruḷichcheytu*" (meaning, He bestowed the radiant feet that remove distress). The concept of "**Prathama shatake vīkṣya vādam**" established by the Vēdānta teachers also confirms this interpretation.

4. The pre-eminence of this holy place can also be understood from the fact that it is commonly referred to as "Perumāḷ Kōyil" (Lord's Temple). While all **Divya Desams** in the world are ultimately the Lord's temples, it is significant that this particular one has been known as "Perumāḷ Kōyil" since ancient times. Many commentaries have emerged for the **Brahmasūtras**, known as **Śārīrakamīmāṁsā**. Other commentaries are referred to by their authors' names, such as **Śaṅkarabhāṣyam**, **Madhvabhāṣyam**, and **Nīlakaṇḍabhāṣyam**. However, the commentary composed by Svāmi Rāmānuja is uniquely known throughout the world as **Śrībhāṣyam**, not "Rāmānujabhāṣyam". Similarly, this place alone is referred to as "Perumāḷ Kōyil."

5. Listen further to the special characteristics of this holy place. When people from outside come here, they say they are going to "Perumāḷ Kōyil". When they arrive and proceed from the entrance to the Sannidhi, they say they are going to "Kōyil". After entering the temple, when they proceed to worship Śrī Hastigiri Shikharojjvala Pārijāta, they say they are going to "Tirumalai". Thus, it is appropriate to understand that the three **Divya Desams** – Kōyil, Tirumalai, Perumāḷ Kōyil – are all present here.
