**A few draft rules filed by the 30th, 31st and 32nd Defendants.**

During Puja, in the whole of the first and second asanas, the Upanishads should be recited.

Mantrapushpam should be recited in the third asana.

After the close of Mantrapushpam and immediately after Ghatadeepam or Karpurarati, the Tengalais and Vadagalais should sit and perform Sevakalam in the under-mentioned manner.

In daily worship no Satumurai can be done.

In the night time, during Puja, Vedaparayanam, Mantra-pushpam and Prabandham Sevakalam should be done in the same way as in the day time.

On Satumurai occasions of Alwars and Acharyas, after the Vadagalais finish the Satumurai of Vedam and Mantra-pushpam, the Tengalais and Vadagalais should recite the last two stanzas of the Prabandham which they were reciting during Puja.

During processions that run as far as the Tiruvadi Koil, Iyal or Sevakalam should commence in the front of the Tiruvadi Koil and close on the northern side of the front four-pillared mantapam in the courtyard of the temple.

During Madaveedi processions, Iyal should commence in the western corner of Varaguvasal Street and close on the northern side of the said four-pillared mantapam.

On the first festival day alone of Brahmotsavam, Sevakalam should commence close to the sacred tank inside the temple and finish in front of the Tiruvadi Koil and again commence at the entrance of West Raja Street and finish in front of the Iyal Satu Mantapam.
